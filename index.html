Advisory Bike Lanes

This bike lane is on the street and divided with a dashed line. Cars and busses are permitted to drive on the lane. Parking vehicles is not allowed.

Bike Lanes

Bike lanes are on-road lanes marked with a solid line and a road sign. You have to use this lane as a bicyclist. Therefore all motorized traffic is excluded and not permitted to drive or park there.

Bus Lanes

Bus lanes are special lanes only for busses and taxis. You are allowed to use this lane as a bicyclist, when marked with a extra road sign. Bus lanes can either have dashed or solid lines.

Cycle Tracks/Paths

Cycle tracks are dedicated lanes seperated from the road. Usually cycle tracks are located on the sidewalk. They are either shared with pedestrians, seperated trough painted marks, structurally differentiated or seperated.

Protected Bike Lanes

Protected bike lanes are new in Germany. They are physically separated lanes dedicated to bikes only.

Parking Security

The times for bicycle thieves are to be harder in Berlin due to the case numbers in bicycle theft remain at a hight level. According to crime statistics. Recently, the thieves have read on high-quality e-bikes. The diagram shows the relationship between the rate of raid cases and enlightened cases of bicycle theft by police. Obviously, that the dark figure is likely to be much higher. And, that about every sixth stolen bike was left unsecured. Wheels were often stolen in the public area, such as at train stations, in front of schools or shopping malls, and that theft from enclosed spaces such as garages, basements, and retail spaces, is only a very small proportion. The bicycle theft is partly favored by a missing or a bad backup (cheap locks).

It is quite clear that more and more Berliners are cycling in the city. But there is a lack of parking spaces, especially at train stations. Berlin senate is planning to build bicycle parking garages and bicycle stations at train stations such as Ostkreuz, Hauptbahnhof, Südkreuz, Zoo or Gesundbrunnen or somewhere.

In Bernau there is the first bicycle parking garage in the region. There are lockable bicycle parking box, only a few locations. A parking box with 10 doors around. You can book your „Bicycle-hotel“ with velo-easy app to pay 50 cent for 1 hour.

What is the city planning?

The data visualizations about the security make obvious that Berlin is not a cycle-friendly city. Especially the problematic and even partly not existing cycle paths are a risky business for cyclists. There is urgent need for action. Therefore, the city has huge plans to improve those bad conditions. The so-called „Radgesetz“ (translated: bikelaw) includes the construction of a gapless bicycle network system. It provides: broad cycle paths on main roads; side roads consisting of bicycle boulevards and where not possible at least side roads which are qualified and clearly identified for cycle traffic; bicycle highways and distance routes. (fahrradparken)The bicycle network system is planned to be finished by the year 2030 (vgl. Dok. Referententwurf Mobilitätsgesetz).

Planned Paths

General Bike Paths

From year 2018 to 2020 60 new bike paths, cycle lanes and further improvements of the bicycle infrastructure are planned for all districts in Berlin. There are up to eight construction measures per district. Especially Treptow-Köpenick (eight construction plans), Charlottenburg-Wilmersdorf and Lichtenberg (seven each) will have meaningful improvements in the bicycle infrastructure. It is noticeable that many main roads will get a bike lane (vgl. https://www.berliner-zeitung.de/berlin/verkehr/mobilitaetsgesetz-berlin-bekommt-60-neue-radwege-bis-2020-29512972).

Cycle Highways

The politicians agreed that 100 kilometers of cycle highways will be built in Berlin. They are planned to improve the connection between the suburbs and the city center for cyclists and therefore they should make the switch over from car to bicycle more attractive. Cycle highways are supposed to be broad enough, safe and preferably without crossroads. The senate submitted a list of eight cycle highways routes. „new“ protected bike lanes

One focal long-term goal of the bike law are the protected bike lanes to increase both the objective as well as subjective traffic safety. Protected bike lanes are very new in Germany but exist in many countries worldwide already (e.g. USA, Spain, France). They are built on the street and are separated from the moving traffic, for instance by bollards or flower tubs (vgl. Referentenentwurf).

Berlin will be the first municipality in Germany which is introducing the protected bike lanes. The implementation of its construction is planned to start this year (2018). The terms are that…
… they should be at least 2 meters broad
… there is a blocked area of 1 meter between bicycle and motorized traffic
… e.g. bollards (0,9 meters high) prevent cars to use the cycle path
… there will be also a visual separation from the roadway by a green coloring of the cycle

(source: berlin.de, 29.01.2018)

Visions Berlin

Besides certain plans that are expected to be implemented in the near future, there are also some visionary concepts developed to help Berlin becoming a cycle-friendly city.

Currently the biggest vision about bicycling in Berlin is the „Radbahn“. The concept aims to realize the best bike path worldwide in the centre of Berlin. It wants to transform the broadly unused space under and along the elevated viaduct of the metro U1 into a vibrant urban space with 9 km of comfortable roofed bike path attached with many social, innovative and recreation spaces.

(source: radbahn.berlin, 29.01.2018)
