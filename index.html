Bicycling in Berlin

the transformation into a cycle-friendly city

By Alexander Käßner, Jolanta Paliszewska, Caroline Zygmunt.
A cooperation between FH Potsdam and FixMyBerlin.

Bicyclists belong as well as further vehicle drivers and pedestrians equally to the traffic. All traffic participants have the essential right for safety. In Berlin the condition of the bicycle infrastructure seems to be unsatisfactory. According to this,

the association Changing Cities e.V. instigated the „Volksentscheid Fahrrad” (translated: plebiscite bicycle) which is an initiative for a safe and convenient bicycle infrastructure in Berlin.

Caused by the very successful initiative the official draft for the „Radgesetz” (translated: bike law) as part of the „Mobilitätsgesetz” (translated: mobility law) was formed for Berlin as the first state in Germany in 2017. Based on it, the aims are to increase the bicycle traffic as well as the „Vision Zero” which focuses on reducing the number of traffic deaths to zero.[1]

To implement the ideas of the bike law draft and to reach the targeted objectives it will require a lot commitment. This website informs you about Berlins current cycle network, the factual and subjective cycling safety as well as future plans and ideas. At the end it will give you hints of how you can contribute to bring Berlin one step closer to become a cycle-friendly city.

How safe is bicycling in Berlin?

At first sight, Berlin has quite a lot of bike lanes and cycle tracks. Most of the existing cycle tracks are structurally separated, e.g. by different cobblestones. However,

there are still plenty of gaps in the cycle path network.

For instance, huge parts of Neukölln and Mitte and are completely without bike paths. In fact, there is no district with a gapless system. In addition, it seems that especially the secondary roads of Berlin are disregarded.

The accident statistics clearly show that bicycling is a risky business in Berlin. Cyclists suffer from accidents by far the most of all traffic participants. In 2016 almost all of the 17 fatal bicycle accidents happened in consequence of collisions with trucks or cars. Mainly because the bicyclist was overlooked while turning.

Besides all the accidents statistics, there are a lot of small and “almost” accidents which are not registered and therefore not countable.
But do the bicyclists in Berlin actually also feel unsafe on the streets? The German newspaper “Berliner Morgenpost” created a survey about that. Over 1800 readers of an article about bike security took part.

The survey shows that more than 60% feel unsafe riding their bike in Berlin. This result is alarming as

subjective safety is essential for improving the ride quality and for bringing more people to choose the bike over car.

Increasing the number of bicyclists is one goal of the mobility law to improve and relieve the whole traffic system.

This map shows the locations and numbers of bicycle accidents in Berlin. It is clearly visible that a lot of accidents happen on roads without any bike paths (e.g. Neukölln, Mitte or Charlottenburg). One of the reasons for accidents on bike paths and junctions is the confusing or simply bad traffic routing for bicyclists.

The most named spots are marked on the map. The shown locations come along with a description of their particular problems within the bike infrastructure. Those problems can be applied to multiple places in the city.

Parking Security

The times for bicycle thieves are to be harder in Berlin due to the case numbers in bicycle theft remain at a hight level. According to crime statistics. Recently, the thieves have read on high-quality e-bikes. The diagram shows the relationship between the rate of raid cases and enlightened cases of bicycle theft by police. Obviously the dark figure is likely to be much higher. Also due to the fact, that about every sixth stolen bike was left unsecured. Wheels were often stolen in the public area, such as at train stations, in front of schools or shopping malls, whereas theft from enclosed spaces such as garages, basements, and retail spaces, is only a very small proportion. The bicycle theft is partly favored by a missing or a bad backup (cheap locks).

It is quite clear that more and more Berliners are cycling in the city. But there is a lack of parking spaces, especially at train stations. Berlin senate is planning to build bicycle parking garages and bicycle stations at train stations such as Ostkreuz, Hauptbahnhof, Südkreuz, Zoo or Gesundbrunnen or somewhere.

In Bernau there is the first bicycle parking garage in the region. There are lockable bicycle parking box, but there are only a few locations, yet. A parking box with 10 doors around. You can book your „Bicycle-hotel“ with velo-easy app to pay 50 cent for 1 hour.

What is the city planning?

The data visualizations about the security make obvious that Berlin is not a cycle-friendly city. Especially the problematic and even partly not existing cycle paths are a risky business for cyclists. There is urgent need for action. Therefore, the city has huge plans to improve those bad conditions.

«The so-called „Radgesetz“ (translated: bikelaw) includes the construction of a gapless bicycle network system.»

It provides: broad cycle paths on main roads; side roads consisting of bicycle boulevards and where not possible at least side roads which are qualified and clearly identified for cycle traffic; bicycle highways and distance routes. The bicycle network system is planned to be finished by the year 2030.[2]

Planned Paths

General Bike Paths

From year 2018 to 2020 60 new bike paths, cycle lanes and further improvements of the bicycle infrastructure are planned for all districts in Berlin. There are up to eight construction measures per district. Especially Treptow-Köpenick (eight construction plans), Charlottenburg-Wilmersdorf and Lichtenberg (seven each) will have meaningful improvements in the bicycle infrastructure. It is noticeable that many main roads will get a bike lane.[3]

Cycle Highways

The politicians agreed that 100 kilometers of cycle highways will be built in Berlin. They are planned to improve the connection between the suburbs and the city center for cyclists and therefore they should make the switch over from car to bicycle more attractive. Cycle highways are supposed to be broad enough, safe and preferably without crossroads. The senate submitted a list of eight cycle highways routes. „new“ protected bike lanes

One focal long-term goal of the bike law are the protected bike lanes to increase both the objective as well as subjective traffic safety. Protected bike lanes are very new in Germany but exist in many countries worldwide already (e.g. USA, Spain, France). They are built on the street and are separated from the moving traffic, for instance by bollards or flower tubs.[5]

Berlin will be the first municipality in Germany which is introducing the protected bike lanes. The implementation of its construction is planned to start this year (2018). The terms are that…
… they should be at least 2 meters broad
… there is a blocked area of 1 meter between bicycle and motorized traffic
… e.g. bollards (0,9 meters high) prevent cars to use the cycle path
… there will be also a visual separation from the roadway by a green coloring of the cycle

(source: berlin.de, 29.01.2018)

Visions Berlin

Besides certain plans that are expected to be implemented in the near future, there are also some visionary concepts developed to help Berlin becoming a cycle-friendly city.

Currently the biggest vision about bicycling in Berlin is the „Radbahn“. The concept aims to realize the best bike path worldwide in the centre of Berlin. It wants to transform the broadly unused space under and along the elevated viaduct of the metro U1 into a vibrant urban space with 9 km of comfortable roofed bike path attached with many social, innovative and recreation spaces.

(source: radbahn.berlin, 29.01.2018)
